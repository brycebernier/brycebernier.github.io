Nutrition Basics for Beginners

You don't need a strict diet plan to see results. The goal of this page is to help you understand how food fuels your body, make smarter everyday choices, and build sustainable eating habits that work alongside your fitness routine — not against it.

Macronutrients

Your body relies on three primary macronutrients: protein (muscle repair and growth), carbohydrates (your main energy source), and fats (hormone support and satiety). Aim to include all three at most meals in portions that feel satisfying, not stuffed. For most beginners, a rough target of 30% protein, 40% carbs, and 30% fat is a solid starting point.

Portion Guidelines

A simple way to estimate portions without tracking every gram is to use your hand as a guide. Use a palm-sized portion of protein, a cupped hand of starchy carbs, a thumb of healthy fats, and as many vegetables as you want filling the rest of your plate. For most people, this approach at two to three meals per day is enough to support beginner fitness goals.

Hydration & Snacks

Staying properly hydrated supports your energy levels, focus, and muscle recovery. A general guideline is to aim for at least half your bodyweight in ounces of water per day. For snacks, choose combinations that include protein and fiber — options like Greek yogurt with fruit, a hard-boiled egg with vegetables, or a handful of nuts with an apple will keep you full longer.

Pre- and Post-Workout Nutrition

What you eat around your workouts matters. Before a workout, try to eat a small meal with carbohydrates and some protein 1–2 hours beforehand — something like oatmeal with a boiled egg or a banana with peanut butter. After your workout, prioritize protein within 30–60 minutes to help your muscles recover and rebuild. For more science-backed guidance, visit the Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health Nutrition Source.

Combine Nutrition with Consistent Training

Small, consistent improvements to your eating habits compound over time and dramatically improve your workout results, energy, and overall wellbeing.
